> **202** Days to Prepare. North-Holland, The Netherlands. 
{: .prompt-info } 

Today I had another great training session, this time I weighed my backpack and improved it by attaching a few pouches and my bidon to its belt! <br />
This was an instant improvement, because I could easily reach my water and phone. I also started my pedometer at the start of the hike and these are the results: I put 8 kg of weight in the form of dumbbells in my backpack making for a total weight of 13.8 kg excluding a half litre of water. I've walked 5 km in 1 hr and 6 minutes with an average speed of 4.5 km/h. <br />
The route I took was a bit more adventurous with a few minor elevation and dirt paths.


![Small Trunk Bridge](/assets/pics/20250120_OhMyWhatObstacles.jpg){: .w-50 .shadow .rounded-10 }
_Small trunk-bridge_


I also took off one of my undershirts, which made my back a whole lot less sweaty. Something I do need to look out for is that I have the tendency to put most of my stuff on the left side of my backpack, making it a bit unbalanced. This caused my left shoulder and neck muscles to be sore more quickly. <br />
And good walking boots is something I will need to invest in!


I'm also in the process of getting singing lessons and trying to teach myself how to play the harmonica. I figured this might be a nice way to make a little extra cash along the way. 


Tomorrow I'll join my mother's hiking group, as they have planned a hike through the dunes in Schoorl. I'm a bit nervous because they are seasoned hikers and walk at a steady pace of (at least) 5 km/h!


But I'm looking forward to the extra difficulty the hike and speed will bring! <br />
All in all, another great day! <br />
*Cheers,* <br />

I-Jun
