While hiking I found a great walking stick!

Luckily I found it right near my house, 
so taking it along with me was no trouble at all.
While I was walking I thought of a modification to the walking stick; 
what if I try to make it into a camping seat? 
A three legged stool, 
with a triangle seating.

Below is a sketch of what my plan is.

> <i class='fas fa-person-digging'></i> Image and Updates coming soon. <br/>
{: .prompt-warning }

1. Cut the stick into three equal pieces.
2. Bore a hole on either end, to fit a bolt
3. Add a bolt to one side of each stool-leg.
    To attach the pieces back together.


4. Get a strong piece of fabric 
    (research this)
5. Fold a square of the fabric
    into an equal sided triangle.
6. Punch eyelets into the fabric on each corner.
    Big enough for the flat bolts to fit into.


7. Get a backpack strap and clip buckle
    This will go around the center of the legs, 
    tying them together.


8. Get a backpack strap
    The length of which should be measured
    once the seat is being assembled, 
    so you can see the distance of the legs, 
    from the triangle of the seat.
9. Punch eyelets where the leg-bolt meets the strap.
    Make sure the strap is under tension, 
    so the seat is not too strained.


10. Reattach the legs to make a walking stick.
11. Make bolt holes to attach the seat and straps, 
    so the stool has all its components.
